Stephen

"Working for Yourself"

Spending hours at a time on a bus is a great way to get to know someone quick, and I'm glad that I ended up sitting next to Stephen on my trip throughout Denmark.

Between uncomfortable naps on the cramped bus we chatted about everything from camping in national parks to possible solutions to the housing crisis in Silicon Valley, but our conversation about the entrepreneurial spirit really stood out to me.

Stephen told me about "How I Made This", a podcast centred around the stories of innovators, entrepreneurs, and idealists, and talked about Yvon Chouinard, the founder of Patagonia. Patagonia was build around Yvon's drive to create products that he would actually use, and Stephen and I both admire that drive. Working towards personal fulfillment is something a lot of people dismiss in favour of stability.

Talking to Stephen about blazing your own trail and allowing yourself the freedom to pursue passions strengthened my resolve to break out on my own after graduation. Sure, there's the fear of failure, and the nagging doubts that will accompany risk taking, but the more that I think about it the more unsure I become about finding satisfaction in the traditional workplace.

Chasing after the entrepreneurial dream is seen by many as foolhardy. Nowadays, it seems to me that spending the majority of your working life doing things that you don't want to be doing is the bigger mistake.
