Anthony

"Joy in the Work"

Energy. If nothing else, Anthony was all about energy. I met him while I was grabbing breakfast at a newly opened cafe in Budapest and within the minute he had struck up a conversation with me about his bold plans for the cafe.

Cafe des Arts & Innovation is in the centre of Budapest, a five minute walk from a nightlife strip, and ten minutes away from the famous ruin bars. More interesting than that, Anthony's vision of it included a co-working space on the first floor (complete with 3D-printers), a cozy cafe vibe on the ground floor, and a hidden bar in the basement, all decked out in a sci-fi, gadget theme. His eyes lit up as he bounced from idea to idea, gesturing at the potential greatness that he envisioned.

Anthony's enthusiasm for his project quickly got me excited about it as well. He was so passionate about it that I was shocked when he told me that he actually didn't have any stake in the business. He just walked into the cafe, saw it's potential, and spoke to the owners about lending a hand in renovating the place.

Working hard on a project simply for the joy of creation is refreshing to see. It seems like a lot of the people around me are so focused on getting a degree so that they can get a job so that they can make money so that they can... what? Meeting Anthony was a reminder that not everything in life needs to have a well defined reward. Enjoying what you do is a reason enough to do it.

Energy may have been the best one word descriptor for Anthony, but if I was allowed to use a phrase I could paint a better picture: love what you do and you'll never work a day in your life.
